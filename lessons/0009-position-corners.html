Rubik's Cube · Lesson 9

Positioning the Last Corners

The home stretch. Get each corner to its right address first — never mind which way it's facing.

From Lesson 8: matched yellow cross — every side shows that inverted-T of solid color. The only unsolved pieces on the whole cube are the four top corners. White stays down, yellow up.

The Big Idea

Split the corner job in two. First, send each corner to its correct home (the spot between its three matching faces) — ignoring how it's twisted. Next lesson, you twist them. Place first, face later.

Place vs face — your oldest idea, last appearance. Since Lesson 1 you've known a piece can be in the right spot but turned the wrong way. Corners are the purest case: a corner can sit in its exact home and still be twisted. So we solve position now and save orientation (the twist) for the finale. And the tool is the same 3-cycle you just used on the edges — now aimed at corners.

Read it: which corner is already "home"?

A corner is home when its three colors are the three colors of the faces meeting at that spot — in any rotation. Example: the corner that belongs between the red and green faces (with yellow on top) is the red+green+yellow piece. If that piece is sitting there — even twisted so yellow faces sideways — it's home.1

Looking down at the top. The FRU corner is the one nearest you on the right.
Front = toward you (bottom). The green-outlined corner = FRU (front-right). That's where your already-home corner goes before you run the move.

The tool: a corner 3-cycle

Hold a home corner at FRU (front-right-top). This cycles the other three corners around it, leaving the FRU corner and every edge untouched:1

Corner positioner
U R U' L' U R' U' L
Two cases, one move. If one corner is already home, put it at FRU and run the move (repeat up to ~3×, keeping a home corner at FRU each time, until all four are home). If no corner is home, just run it once from any angle — that creates a home corner — then proceed. It's another pure 3-cycle, so edges and lower layers never move.1

Hands-on: get all four corners home ~5 min

  1. White down, yellow up. Check each top corner: are its 3 colors the trio of the 3 faces around it (any twist counts)?
  2. Found a home corner? Turn the whole cube so it sits at FRU (front-right-top). No home corner? Skip to step 4.
  3. Run U R U' L' U R' U' L. Re-check; repeat with a home corner at FRU until all four are home.
  4. No home corner at all? Run the move once from any angle — now one corner will be home. Go back to step 2.
  5. All four corners home. They're in the right addresses but some are twisted (yellow not on top). That's expected. The very next lesson twists them and finishes the cube.

What you just learned: position before orientation. With every piece finally in its home, only the twists remain.

Heads up — don't expect the top to look "better" yet

After this step the corners are in the right places but likely twisted, so the top will look messy, not more yellow. That's correct. Resist the urge to "fix" it now — the twisting is the next (and final) step. If the corners seem to keep cycling without settling, double-check you're keeping a genuinely home corner at FRU each time.
