The Matched Cross

Your white cross is real — but the second colors don't match yet. Today you fix that, foolproof, and learn the one idea you were missing.

Where you are: you've got a white cross — four white edges making a + on the white face. But you noticed the other color on each edge doesn't line up with the center next to it. Spotting that is the whole skill. Let's make it a true cross.

Why this lesson? A white cross where the side colors are wrong is a fake cross — it looks done but it's built crooked, and every later stage (corners, middle layer) will sit on top of that mistake. Five minutes now saves the whole solve. And the fix teaches the single most important habit on the cube: a piece is only home when ALL its colors are right.
This is "pieces, not stickers" coming back to bite — in a good way. Back in Lesson 1 you learned an edge has two colors and one true home. A white edge isn't done just because its white sticker landed on the white face. Its second color has to meet its matching center. You felt that something was off — that instinct is cube literacy.

The Big Idea

Line up an edge's side color with its matching center first — then bring it straight down. The color was right before it dropped, so it stays right after.

That "match first, drop second" order is the step that was missing. If you bring an edge down to the white face before checking its side color, you're just hoping it lands matched — and usually it doesn't. We're going to do it in the order that can't go wrong.

The foolproof tool: the Daisy 🌼

Set up: hold the cube with yellow center on top, white on the bottom — same orientation as Lesson 4. We'll stage the edges on the open top, match them, then drop them onto the white bottom.1

A daisy is four white edges arranged around the yellow center on top — white stickers pointing up, like petals around a flower. At this stage you ignore the side colors completely; you just gather the white edges up top.

The daisy — looking down at the top face. Yellow center, four white "petals."

To build the daisy, just hunt down the four white edges (wherever they're hiding) and turn faces until each one sits as a white petal on top. Already have a white cross on the bottom? Then you're almost there — for each bottom edge, turn its side face 180° to lift it up into a petal, and now you can re-place it the right way. (Don't worry about wrecking the cross — we're rebuilding it correctly.)

Now drop each petal — matched

One petal at a time:

  1. Pick a petal and look at its side color (the sticker on the side face, not the white on top).
  2. Turn just the top layer (U) until that side color sits on the same face as its matching center — e.g. a petal with a red side goes directly above the red center.
  3. Now spin that whole face 180° (e.g. F2) to swing the edge straight down to the white bottom. White lands on the bottom; the side color — already matched — stays matched.
  4. Repeat for the other three petals. Each edge goes to a different face, so you never re-turn a face you've already finished — placed edges stay put.
Why this can't collide. The four cross edges each live on a different side (F2, R2, B2, L2 — one per edge). Turning the top to aim the next petal never touches an edge already sitting on the bottom. And a 180° face turn drops the new edge in without flipping it. So once an edge is matched-and-dropped, it's locked — guaranteed. That's the difference between hoping and knowing.

Hands-on: rebuild it as a matched cross ~5 min · true cross DONE

  1. Yellow on top, white on bottom.
  2. Make the daisy — get all four white edges as petals around the yellow center, ignoring side colors for now.
  3. Drop them matched, one by one: look at a petal's side color → U until it's above its same-color center → that face 180° down.
  4. Check your band. Walk around the cube. Every side should show its center color continuing straight down into the cross edge — four matched columns, like the green diagram above.
  5. True cross complete. White + on the bottom, and all four side colors matched. Now the foundation is square.

What you just locked in: the habit that runs the entire rest of the solve — match the colors first, commit the piece second.

Snag — a white edge has its white sticker on the SIDE, not on top

That edge is "flipped." Don't force it down — you'd get white pointing sideways (card 3 above). Park it up in the top layer first so its white faces up as a proper petal, then match-and-drop it. If it's stuck on the bottom flipped, lift it with a 180° of its side face and re-place it as a petal.
