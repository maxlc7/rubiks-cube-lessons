Rubik's Cube · Lesson 10 · The Finish

Twisting the Corners

Every piece is home. One tiny move, repeated, twists the last corners yellow — and the cube solves itself in your hands.

From Lesson 9: all four top corners are in their correct homes. Some are still twisted — yellow facing sideways instead of up. That twist is the very last thing standing between you and a solved cube. Keep yellow on top, white on the bottom.

The Big Idea

Twist each corner in place until its yellow faces up — one corner at a time, always in the same front-right-top spot. Bring the next twisted corner there by turning only the top. On the last twist, the whole cube clicks to solved.

Place, then face — the very last time. Since Lesson 1 you've separated where a piece is from which way it faces. Lesson 9 put the corners in the right place. This lesson fixes the facing. That's the whole cube in one idea, and you're about to close it out.

The move: a corner-twister

Hold the cube normally — yellow up, white down, a colored face toward you. The corner you're fixing sits at the top-front-right (the upper-right corner of the face you're looking at). The move is just four turns:1

The corner twister
R' D' R D
R′ = right face back · D′ = bottom back · R = right face · D = bottom — repeat as a unit

You do R' D' R D over and over, watching only that top-front-right corner. Each round nudges its yellow toward the top. When yellow lands on top, you stop and move on.

Read this before you start — it will look like you're destroying the cube

While you repeat R' D' R D, the bottom two layers will look completely scrambled. This is normal. Do not stop. Do not try to fix it. Keep going and it always snaps back.

Three rules that make it work every time:

  • Only twist the corner at top-front-right. Watch that one corner, ignore the chaos elsewhere.
  • When that corner's yellow is on top, do not rotate the whole cube — turn only the top (U) to bring the next un-yellow corner to top-front-right.
  • Never leave a corner half-done. Each corner needs the move repeated 2 or 4 times to land yellow-up. Finish it (yellow up) before moving the top.

The full routine

  1. Yellow up, white down. Find a corner whose yellow is not on top and turn the whole cube so it's at top-front-right.
  2. Repeat R' D' R D, watching that corner, until its yellow is on top (2 or 4 times). The cube will look wrecked mid-way — keep going.
  3. Turn only the top (U) to bring the next un-yellow corner to top-front-right. Do not rotate the whole cube.
  4. Repeat the twister on that corner until its yellow is up.
  5. Keep going for all four corners. As you finish the last one and turn the top to line it up — the entire cube solves. 🎉
Why the chaos comes back. R' D' R D only really changes the corner sitting at top-front-right — everything else it disturbs, it later puts back (repeat it 6 times from solved and you're back to solved). Because you only ever turn the top between corners — never the whole cube, never the bottom on its own — each finished corner returns to a finished spot. When the last twist lands, every borrowed piece is home. That's why it looks broken right up until the instant it's perfect.1

Hands-on: solve the cube ~5 min · THE FINISH

  1. Un-yellow corner to top-front-right.
  2. R' D' R D until its yellow is up (ignore the mess).
  3. U to bring the next un-yellow corner to top-front-right.
  4. Repeat until all four are yellow — the cube finishes itself on the last one.

When the last corner clicks: six solid faces. You solved a Rubik's Cube — and you can explain why every stage worked.

Snag — it ended scrambled, not solved

Almost always one of two things: you rotated the whole cube between corners (only ever turn the top, U), or you stopped a corner half-twisted and moved on. Both break the "everything comes back" guarantee. If it's scrambled, no worries — you have all ten lessons now; start again from the white cross.
